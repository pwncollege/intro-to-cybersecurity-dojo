All hosts are constrained by limited resources such as CPU, memory, and bandwidth. A **Denial of Service (DoS)** attack occurs when those constraints are deliberately overwhelmed, preventing the system from delivering its intended *service* to legitimate users. A **Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)** extends this concept by coordinating multiple machines to amplify the impact.

DoS attacks commonly exploit internal resource exhaustion or, as in this challenge, network bandwidth saturation.
